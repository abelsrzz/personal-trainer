# Block 4: Specific 10k Development

## Dates

- `2026-10-27` to `2026-12-21`
- Duration: `8 weeks`

## Block Objective

- Convert aerobic and threshold gains into 10k-specific performance.
- Improve rhythm control, speed endurance and race economy.
- Build specificity from the checkpoint pace band, not from the declared dream target.

## Primary Stimulus

- 10k-oriented intervals at current realistic 10k effort.
- Longer repetitions with controlled recoveries.
- Mixed threshold plus 10k sessions.
- Occasional tune-up race or time trial if it fits recovery.

## Weekly Volume Guide

- Typical range: `58-68 km/week`.
- Repeatable peak range: `68-72 km/week` when recovery and shin response stay excellent.
- Exceptional upper edge: `72-75 km/week` only if months of data show stable recovery and no shin reaction.
- Total load should not rise sharply when specificity rises.

## Key Session Types

- Repetitions such as `5-6 x 1000 m`, `4-5 x 1200 m`, `3-4 x 1600 m`, `3 x 2000 m` or `2 x 3000 m`.
- Mixed sessions such as threshold volume first, then shorter 10k rhythm reps.
- Long run with moderate finish only when weekly recovery is clearly good.
- Short economy work around 3k-5k feel in small doses, never as a weekly addiction.

## Practical Rules

- First target the pace the athlete can repeat, then progress it.
- Favor flat or gently rolling routes similar to the `S` race profile.
- Use plated/race shoes selectively to test economy, not to mask overload.
- Keep one day after demanding sessions genuinely easy.
- Do not increase total volume and specific-session density aggressively in the same week.

## Checkpoints

- Week 4-5: specific session check, for example longer reps at current 10k effort with stable form and recovery.
- Week 7-8: controlled 5k, 8k-10k tune-up or equivalent workout to set the Block 5 target band.
- To keep `35:00` alive as a realistic target, the athlete should be trending toward sub-18 5k ability and controlled longer reps well under `3:50/km` by the end of this block.

## Success Criteria

- Specific sessions become more repeatable.
- Current 10k pace clearly improves from the initial `4:22-4:26/km` reference band.
- The final target range becomes evidence-based.
- Quality improves without sacrificing the easy aerobic base.
- The athlete reaches the end of the block with multiple absorbed weeks above `60 km` if the body allows it.

## Failure Triggers

- Repeated inability to recover from specific work.
- Shin reacts to faster turnover.
- Weekly plan starts revolving around surviving sessions instead of adapting to them.
- Race-pace ambition disconnects from current checkpoint evidence.
