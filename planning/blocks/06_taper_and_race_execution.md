# Block 6: Taper And Race Execution

## Dates

- `2027-01-25` to `2027-02-06`
- Duration: `2 weeks`

## Block Objective

- Preserve sharpness while removing fatigue.
- Arrive fresh, healthy and clear on pacing.
- Execute the `S` race according to the final evidence-based target.

## Primary Stimulus

- Reduced volume.
- Small doses of race-specific rhythm.
- High recovery quality.
- Race logistics and pacing rehearsal.

## Weekly Volume Guide

- Week of `2027-01-25`: around `70-80%` of normal volume, depending on fatigue.
- Race week: low volume before race day, usually `18-28 km` before the race depending on the final normal-week volume.
- Keep intensity touches, but cut total work.

## Key Session Types

- Short race-pace reminders with generous recovery.
- Light neuromuscular strides.
- Easy runs with strict recovery control.
- One short session early in race week, never a fitness test.

## Practical Rules

- No heroic sessions.
- No last-minute target upgrades.
- Prioritize sleep, routine and logistics.
- Confirm pacing strategy, warm-up and race shoes.
- If the shin becomes sensitive, cut volume first and preserve only tiny rhythm reminders.

## Success Criteria

- Fresh legs.
- Stable shin.
- Confidence in the final strategy.
- Clear warm-up, pacing and fueling plan.

## Failure Triggers

- Last-minute hard training.
- Panic adjustments after one bad session.
- Any avoidable shin flare in race week.
- Treating taper as a chance to prove fitness instead of absorb it.
