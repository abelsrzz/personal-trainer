# Initial Planning Guidelines

## Main Constraint

- Protect the left shin and avoid fast load spikes.
- The plan must build from the May 2026 evidence, not from the February 2027 dream pace.

## Current Objective

- Rebuild consistent training from an unstable short-term state after the Camino fatigue and recent shin risk.
- Convert the previous `30-45 km/week` history into a repeatable base before chasing high-specificity sessions.
- Keep `35:00` as an aspirational ceiling until checkpoints justify it.

## Practical Consequences

- Prioritize controlled aerobic work and weekly repeatability.
- Use only two quality slots at most, matching Tuesday and Thursday preferences.
- Keep the long run on Sunday when possible.
- Use heart rate as the main control tool outside interval and tempo sessions.
- Do not prescribe `3:30/km` work until race-specific evidence supports it.
- Be careful with aggressive plated shoes while shin discomfort is active.
- Remove strides, hills or faster running if shin pain rises above `2/10` or is worse the next morning.
