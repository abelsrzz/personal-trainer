2. `Block 2: Aerobic base and volume consolidation`

   - Focus: increase weekly volume and aerobic durability.

3. `Block 3: Threshold development and strength endurance`
   - Dates: `2026-08-17` to `2026-10-11`
   - Focus: improve sustainable speed without high anaerobic cost.

4. `Block 4: Specific 10k development`
   - Dates: `2026-10-12` to `2026-12-20`
   - Focus: convert aerobic gains into 10k-specific work.

5. `Block 5: Competitive sharpening`
   - Dates: `2026-12-21` to `2027-01-24`
   - Focus: sharpen race pace economy and validate realistic target pace.

6. `Block 6: Taper and race execution`
   - Dates: `2027-01-25` to `2027-02-06`
   - Focus: reduce fatigue and arrive fresh to the `S` race.

## Volume Progression

- Current base: `30-40 km/week`.
- Early target after Block 1: `35-45 km/week` if the shin is stable.
- Mid-cycle target during Blocks 2-3: `45-60 km/week` depending on tolerance.
- Specific phase target during Blocks 4-5: maintain the highest sustainable load, not the highest possible load.
- Every 3-4 weeks should include a lighter week if fatigue, soreness or shin symptoms trend up.

## Weekly Structure Principles

1. `Tuesday`
   - Main quality day when recovered.

2. `Thursday`
   - Secondary quality day or controlled aerobic stimulus.

3. `Sunday`
   - Long run day by preference.

4. `Strength`
   - Keep one session per week minimum, adjusted to fatigue and shin status.

5. `Recovery`
   - Easy running and heart-rate caps matter more than forcing pace on tired days.

## Checkpoints

1. End of Block 1
   - Shin symptoms stable or improving.
   - Able to train consistently for 4 weeks.

2. Mid Block 2
   - Weekly volume is rising without accumulating pain.
   - Long run is moving beyond 12 km comfortably.

3. End of Block 3
   - Enough data to estimate threshold pace.
   - Short races or sessions suggest whether the final target remains realistic.

4. End of Block 4
   - A controlled 5k or specific session should define realistic 10k pace bands.

5. End of Block 5
   - Final race strategy and target pace locked in.

## Calibration Rule

- If the body responds well, later weekly plans can push toward the declared `35:00` target.
- If the shin, recovery or checkpoints disagree, the plan should protect continuity first and revise the race target second.
